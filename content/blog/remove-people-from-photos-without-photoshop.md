<section id="the-problem">
  <h2>Why Removing People from Photos Is Hard Without the Right Tool</h2>

  <p>The core challenge in removing a person from a photo is not the deletion — it is what happens to the space they occupied. Every pixel the person covered needs to be replaced with something that matches the surrounding background in texture, color, lighting, and pattern. Done manually in Photoshop, that means selecting, cloning, patching, and blending — a process that can take 20–60 minutes per person and requires experience to produce results that do not look edited.</p>

  <p>Three specific scenarios make this especially difficult without Photoshop skills:</p>

  <div class="two-columns">
    <div>
      <h3>Scenarios That Make Manual Removal Hard</h3>
      <ul class="custom-list">
        <li><strong>Crowded backgrounds</strong> — when the person overlaps with other subjects, every edge requires precise masking to avoid cutting into the wrong element</li>
        <li><strong>Complex textures behind the subject</strong> — cobblestone, water, foliage, and patterned architecture cannot be reconstructed by simple cloning without visible repetition artifacts</li>
        <li><strong>Multiple people</strong> — each person removed exposes the area behind them, which may require its own reconstruction based on what was visible in adjacent frames</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div>
      <h3>What AI Solves</h3>
      <ul class="custom-list">
        <li>Analyzes the entire image to understand texture, lighting, and background patterns before filling</li>
        <li>Generates a plausible reconstruction of the covered area rather than copying from a nearby patch</li>
        <li>Handles edge detection automatically — no manual masking required</li>
        <li>Produces results in 2–5 seconds that would take an experienced Photoshop user 15–40 minutes</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  <p>With multiple people scattered across a cobblestone plaza in front of an architectural subject, the AI Cleanup method is the right choice. The background behind each person is a consistent stone texture — exactly the type of surface that the AI reconstructs most accurately.</p>

<section id="pro-tips">
  <h2>Pro Tips for Best Results</h2>

  <div class="two-columns">
    <div>
      <h3>For Removal (AI Cleanup)</h3>
      <ul class="custom-list">
        <li><strong>Include the shadow.</strong> Brush over the person's ground shadow as well as their body — leaving an isolated shadow after removal immediately looks unnatural</li>
        <li><strong>Work from edges inward.</strong> Remove people near the frame edges first, where surrounding context is clearest, then move to the center</li>
        <li><strong>Remove in batches.</strong> For crowds, remove 2–3 people per pass rather than all at once — the AI has more intact background context to work with for each removal</li>
        <li><strong>Re-run specific patches.</strong> If one area of the reconstruction looks off, select only that patch and remove again — the AI generates a different fill each time</li>
        <li><strong>Zoom to 100% before exporting.</strong> Artifacts that are invisible at fit-to-screen view can be visible on a large display or when cropping tightly</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div>
      <h3>For Replacement (AI Replace)</h3>
      <ul class="custom-list">
        <li><strong>Specify light direction.</strong> Include the lighting conditions from the original photo in your description — "soft light from the left" or "overhead midday sun" — so the replacement element matches the scene</li>
        <li><strong>Match the perspective.</strong> Describe the viewing angle if relevant: "viewed from slightly below, consistent with a wide-angle ground-level shot"</li>
        <li><strong>Size and scale matter.</strong> Add scale context: "a bench approximately 1.5 meters wide" rather than just "a bench"</li>
        <li><strong>Iterate on the description.</strong> If the first result is off, adjust one element of the description at a time rather than rewriting it entirely</li>
        <li><strong>Use Cleanup first for crowds, Replace for isolated subjects.</strong> When multiple people need to be removed, clean the crowd first, then use Replace on any specific spot that needs a compositional element</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="pros-cons" style="display:flex; flex-wrap:wrap; gap:20px; margin:20px 0;">

      <ul style="padding-left:18px;">

        <li>Removing people in small groups from crowded photos</li>
        <li>Working from frame edges inward</li>
        <li>Zooming to 100% to review reconstruction quality before exporting</li>

        <li>Re-running specific patches rather than the entire image when one area needs improvement</li>

    <div class="cons" style="flex:1; min-width:280px; padding:18px; background:#fdedec; border:1px solid #f5b7b1; border-radius:10px;">
