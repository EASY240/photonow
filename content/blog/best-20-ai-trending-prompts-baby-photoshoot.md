<section id="how-it-works">
  <h2>How AI Baby Portrait Generation Works</h2>

  <p>AI image generation for infant photography operates through three sequential processes. Understanding each process helps you make better decisions at the prompt-writing stage.</p>

  <div class="three-columns">
    <div class="column-card">
      <h3>Image-to-Image Reference Matching</h3>
      <p>When you upload a reference photo of the baby, the AI uses facial landmark detection to map the infant's facial geometry — eye spacing, nose width, head shape, skin tone. This geometric data is then applied to the generated scene, placing the same face inside the new environment rather than inventing a generic infant face.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="column-card">
      <h3>Environment Synthesis</h3>
      <p>The text prompt defines every element of the generated scene: background architecture, lighting source, props, color palette, depth of field. The more specific the description, the less interpretive freedom the model has — which means fewer generic or inconsistent outputs. Scene synthesis is where structured prompts outperform vague instructions by the widest margin.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="column-card">
      <h3>Skin and Texture Rendering</h3>
      <p>Infant skin has specific optical properties — high subsurface scattering, soft specular reflections, fine texture without visible pores. Generic AI generation produces plastic-looking skin unless these properties are specified explicitly in the prompt. Keywords like "subsurface scattering," "natural skin grain," and "photorealistic baby skin texture" activate the correct rendering mode.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="bg-blue-50 border border-blue-200 rounded-lg p-4 my-4">
    <p class="text-sm text-blue-800"><strong>Semantic context:</strong> Professional AI baby portrait generation draws from the same technical vocabulary as traditional studio photography — lens focal length, aperture, lighting pattern names, color temperature. Understanding these terms lets you write prompts that produce predictably professional results. <section id="step-by-step">
  <h2>Step-by-Step: How to Generate AI Baby Photos</h2>

  <ol class="list-numbers">
    <li>
      <strong>Select your AI image generator.</strong> For themed baby portraits with facial identity retention, use <a href="https://www.midjourney.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Midjourney V6/V7</a> for highest photorealism, <a href="https://www.bing.com/images/create" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Bing Image Creator</a> for free generation with embedded text, or the <a href="https://modernphototools.com/tools/ai-image-generator" target="_blank">ModernPhotoTools AI Image Generator</a> for browser-based generation without an account.
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Prepare the reference photo.</strong> Use a clear, front-facing, well-lit photo of the baby with a neutral expression and minimal background clutter. The sharper and more face-forward the reference, the more accurately the model retains facial features across different scenes. Avoid angled shots, shadowed faces, and heavily filtered selfies.
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Select and paste the structured prompt.</strong> Choose one of the 20 prompts below that matches the theme you want. Paste it into the generator exactly as written, then replace the generic subject description with a specific reference to your reference photo if the platform supports it (e.g., using Midjourney's <code>--cref</code> parameter with the uploaded image URL).
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Specify technical parameters.</strong> Append aspect ratio to every prompt: <code>--ar 4:5</code> for portrait prints, <code>--ar 1:1</code> for social media feed posts, <code>--ar 9:16</code> for Instagram Stories. In platforms that use natural language, include the ratio description in the prompt text itself.
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Iterate on the specific element that failed.</strong> Do not rewrite the full prompt if only one element is wrong. Identify the exact issue — background inaccuracy, wrong skin tone rendering, flat lighting, identity drift — and modify only that component. Targeted iteration is faster and more reliable than full prompt rewrites.
    </li>
    <li>
      <strong>Post-process the final output.</strong> Run the selected image through the <a href="https://modernphototools.com/tools/ai-image-upscaler" target="_blank">AI Image Upscaler</a> to reach print-ready resolution for physical albums or large-format prints. Use the <a href="https://modernphototools.com/tools/ai-filter" target="_blank">AI Filter</a> to apply color grading that matches your brand aesthetic across a series of images.
    </li>
  </ol>

<section id="pro-tips">
  <h2>Professional Tips for Better AI Baby Portrait Results</h2>

  <div class="two-columns">
    <div>
      <h3>Prompt Engineering Tips</h3>
      <ul class="custom-list">
        <li><strong>Specify optical parameters exactly.</strong> "85mm f/1.8" enforces shallow depth of field and creamy bokeh. "100mm macro f/2.8" creates extreme close-up sharpness. Generic lens instructions like "blurry background" produce inconsistent results.</li>
        <li><strong>Name your lighting pattern.</strong> "Rembrandt lighting," "volumetric light rays," and "golden hour rim light" are established photographic terms the model interprets consistently. "Nice lighting" gives the model full creative discretion.</li>
        <li><strong>Engineer skin texture explicitly.</strong> Include "subsurface scattering," "photorealistic skin grain," and "natural pores" to eliminate the artificial, plastic appearance characteristic of unrefined AI generation.</li>
        <li><strong>Add identity retention at the end of every prompt.</strong> "Preserve exact facial identity," "keep original face unchanged," and "no facial modification" placed at the end of a prompt override earlier generalization instructions.</li>
        <li><strong>Use the character reference parameter in Midjourney.</strong> Append <code>--cref [image URL]</code> to maintain consistent facial geometry across multiple themed generations from the same infant.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div>
      <h3>Reference Photo Quality Tips</h3>
      <ul class="custom-list">
        <li><strong>Use a front-facing, well-lit reference.</strong> The cleaner and more face-forward the reference photo, the more accurately the model retains facial features across different scenes. Angled or shadowed faces produce significant identity drift.</li>
        <li><strong>Avoid heavily filtered reference photos.</strong> Instagram filters, skin-smoothing apps, and heavy post-processing distort the facial geometry the model uses for landmark detection.</li>
        <li><strong>Upload the reference at full resolution.</strong> A 500px reference photo gives the model significantly less facial data than a 2000px equivalent — especially for fine details around the eyes and mouth.</li>
        <li><strong>Generate at least 4 variations per prompt.</strong> AI output varies between generations on the same prompt. Multiple outputs consistently surface better results than single generations plus full prompt rewrites.</li>
        <li><strong>Post-process with AI Filter for brand consistency.</strong> Apply the same color grade across a series of themed portraits using the <a href="https://modernphototools.com/tools/ai-filter" target="_blank">AI Filter tool</a> to create visual cohesion across an album or social media series.</li>
      </ul>

  <h2>Common Mistakes to Avoid</h2>

  <div class="pros-cons" style="display:flex; flex-wrap:wrap; gap:20px; margin:20px 0;">
    <div class="pros" style="flex:1; min-width:280px; padding:18px; background:#e9f7ef; border:1px solid #d5f5e3; border-radius:10px;">
      <h3 style="font-weight:700; margin-bottom:12px;">✓ What Works</h3>
      <ul style="padding-left:18px;">
        <li>Specific optical parameters — "85mm f/1.8" not "shallow depth of field"</li>
        <li>Named lighting patterns from photography vocabulary</li>
        <li>Identity retention keywords at the end of every prompt</li>
        <li>Clean front-facing reference photos at full resolution</li>
        <li>Generating 4+ variations before selecting</li>
        <li>Iterating on the specific failing element only</li>
        <li>Post-processing with AI Upscaler for print delivery</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="cons" style="flex:1; min-width:280px; padding:18px; background:#fdedec; border:1px solid #f5b7b1; border-radius:10px;">
      <h3 style="font-weight:700; margin-bottom:12px;">✗ Common Errors</h3>
      <ul style="padding-left:18px;">
        <li>Vague instructions like "cute baby photo" — produces generic algorithmic averages</li>
        <li>Omitting negative prompts — "no plastic skin," "no distortion" prevent common AI artifacts</li>
        <li>Overloading the prompt with incompatible styles — produces prompt drift where critical instructions are ignored</li>
        <li>Using heavily filtered or angled reference photos — causes facial identity drift</li>
        <li>Treating the first output as final without iterating</li>
        <li>Rewriting the full prompt when only one element is wrong</li>
        <li>Not specifying aspect ratio — produces incorrect dimensions for the intended output format</li>
      </ul>
