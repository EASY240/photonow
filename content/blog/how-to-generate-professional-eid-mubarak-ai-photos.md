  <section id="the-problem">
    <h2>The Problem with Traditional Festive Photography</h2>
    <p>
      Traditional festive photography for Eid requires professional studio lighting, staged backgrounds with culturally specific props, and advanced retouching in tools like Photoshop or Lightroom. The result is that most users either accept generic mobile phone photos with flat lighting and messy backgrounds, or spend hours in editing apps making slider adjustments that require trained eye-hand coordination to produce professional results.
    </p>
    <p>
      AI image generation eliminates both constraints — but only when prompts are structured correctly. A vague prompt like "Eid photo of a man in a mosque" produces the same low-specificity problem as an underlit phone photo: the output is technically generated but visually generic, culturally imprecise, and practically unusable as a professional greeting or social post.
    </p>
    <p>
      The solution is the same principle that governs all professional prompt engineering: specificity at every layer. Subject definition, attire naming, lighting source, architectural style, composition, and quality modifiers all need to be explicit. This guide shows exactly what that looks like for Eid visuals. For background replacement on real photos, the <a href="https://modernphototools.com/tools/ai-replace" target="_blank">AI Replace tool</a> handles environment swaps without prompt generation.
    </p>
  </section>

          <h3 style="margin:0 0 6px;color:#1f2937;">Define the Subject</h3>
          <p style="margin:0;color:#555;">Specify demographic details and exact traditional attire. Do not use generic terms like "traditional clothes." Use precise names: <strong>kurta</strong> (South Asian men's collarless shirt), <strong>thobe</strong> (Gulf/Middle Eastern full-length robe), <strong>abaya</strong> (women's full-length outer garment), <strong>shalwar kameez</strong> (tunic and trousers), <strong>anarkali</strong> (flared long dress), <strong>baju kurung</strong> (Malaysian traditional), <strong>kufi</strong> (rounded cap), <strong>sherwani</strong> (formal coat). The more specific the attire name, the more culturally accurate the output.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

        <div>
          <h3 style="margin:0 0 6px;color:#1f2937;">Set the Scene and Lighting</h3>
          <p style="margin:0;color:#555;">Establish the environment (mosque courtyard, rooftop, festive interior, balcony) and specific lighting conditions. Eid visuals use two primary light signatures: <strong>golden hour warm amber</strong> (outdoor, sunset, post-Eid prayer) and <strong>lantern/candlelight warm glow</strong> (evening, interior, intimate). Name the exact light source — "fanoos lantern as primary light source," "gold-plated crescent moon backlight," "warm sunset from the right." Also specify the architectural style: "Ottoman-style mosque," "Mughal mosque," "modern Islamic architecture."</p>
        </div>
      </div>

        <div style="min-width:38px;height:38px;background:#6366f1;color:#fff;border-radius:50%;display:flex;align-items:center;justify-content:center;font-weight:700;font-size:1rem;flex-shrink:0;">4</div>
        <div>
          <h3 style="margin:0 0 6px;color:#1f2937;">Input the Prompt</h3>
          <p style="margin:0;color:#555;">Paste the structured text into the generator's prompt field. For Bing Image Creator, paste directly. For Midjourney, paste into the <code>/imagine</code> command and append parameters like <code>--ar 9:16</code> for stories or <code>--ar 1:1</code> for feed posts. For Gemini, paste as a conversational message. Do not split the prompt across multiple messages — input it as a single block.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

        <div>
          <h3 style="margin:0 0 6px;color:#1f2937;">Generate and Refine</h3>
          <p style="margin:0;color:#555;">Do not restart from zero if the first output is wrong. Identify the exact element that failed — background inaccuracy, wrong attire color, flat lighting, distorted face — and modify only that component of the prompt. Targeted iteration produces faster results than full rewrites.   <section id="pro-tips">
    <h2>Professional Tips</h2>

    <h3>Specify Architectural Styles by Name</h3>
    <p>
      Use precise architectural terms to prevent generic or geographically inaccurate backgrounds. "Ottoman-style mosque" produces a specific visual signature: large central dome, semi-domes, multiple minarets with pointed tips, ornate tile work. "Mughal mosque" produces a different signature: bulbous domes, decorative arched iwans, red sandstone with white marble inlay. "Modern Islamic architecture" produces a third type. Name the style you need — the model will not guess correctly from "mosque" alone. See <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_architecture" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Islamic architecture reference</a> for style terminology.
    </p>

    <h3>Use Exact Garment Names for Cultural Accuracy</h3>
    <p>
      "Traditional clothes" produces random, often inaccurate results across different AI models. Use specific garment names: <strong>kurta</strong> (collarless South Asian shirt), <strong>thobe/dishdasha</strong> (Gulf full-length robe), <strong>sherwani</strong> (formal fitted coat, South Asian), <strong>baju kurung</strong> (Malaysian blouse and skirt set), <strong>anarkali</strong> (flared dress with fitted bodice), <strong>salwar kameez</strong> (tunic and loose trousers). For accessories: <strong>kufi</strong> (rounded cap), <strong>taqiyah</strong> (skull cap), <strong>hijab</strong> (head covering), <strong>niqab</strong> (face veil). Each term produces a culturally distinct visual output.
    </p>

    <h3>Optimize Lighting Using Named Sources</h3>
    <p>
      Named light sources produce predictable, natural-looking light quality. "Fanoos lantern as the primary light source" casts warm amber side-light with geometric shadow patterns. "Gold-plated crescent moon backlight" produces a rim-lit silhouette effect with warm halo. "Warm sunset from camera right" produces directional golden-hour side lighting. Generic terms like "good lighting" or "well-lit" give the model full discretion — which produces inconsistent, often flat results. For real portrait photos, the <a href="https://modernphototools.com/tools/ai-portrait" target="_blank">AI Portrait tool</a> can re-apply lighting corrections post-generation.
    </p>

    <h3>Define Technical Parameters Explicitly</h3>
    <p>
      Always specify aspect ratio (<code>16:9</code> for landscape banners, <code>9:16</code> for stories, <code>1:1</code> for feed posts) and mandate resolution quality using keywords: <strong>"8K resolution"</strong>, <strong>"hyper-realistic"</strong>, <strong>"ultra-detailed"</strong>, <strong>"cinematic"</strong>. Without these modifiers, default output quality is lower and composition is unpredictable. In Midjourney, append <code>--ar 9:16</code> after the prompt. In Bing Creator and Gemini, include the aspect ratio description within the prompt text itself.
    </p>

    <h3>Facial Likeness in Image-to-Image Workflows</h3>
    <p>
      When applying Eid visual context to a specific person's photo, upload a clear, front-facing, well-lit photo with neutral expression and no sunglasses. The cleaner the reference, the more accurately the model retains facial features during outfit and background transformation. In Midjourney, use the <code>--cref [image URL]</code> (character reference) parameter to maintain facial consistency across multiple generations. After generation, use the <a href="https://modernphototools.com/tools/remove-background" target="_blank">Background Remover</a> to isolate the subject and place them precisely on a new Eid background.
    </p>
